ULTRASOUND APPEARANCE ENCODING RULE (MANDATORY WHEN SOURCE SUPPORTS)

If the provided Core/RadPrimer/STATdx source explicitly describes ultrasound appearance
(echogenicity, echotexture, posterior acoustic behavior, Doppler vascularity, compressibility, cystic-versus-solid morphology, source-stated sonographic signs/artifacts, or ultrasound-specific discriminator features):

You MUST generate at least ONE dedicated High-Yield card encoding that ultrasound appearance.

(If Sectioned Build mode is active, this requirement is QUEUED and must be emitted only in SECTION G.)

Rules:
- Ultrasound details may be included ONLY if explicitly stated in the source.
- Include the sonographic feature exactly as written when possible.
- Include Doppler, posterior acoustic behavior, compressibility, mobility, wall layering, maneuver findings, or source-named artifacts/signs ONLY if explicitly stated.
- Do NOT infer Doppler behavior unless source-supported.
- Do NOT infer posterior acoustic enhancement or shadowing unless source-supported.
- Do NOT infer compressibility unless source-supported.
- Do NOT infer wall stratification unless source-supported.
- Do NOT infer mobility, sludge/debris behavior, twinkle, ring-down, comet-tail, reverberation, or other ultrasound-specific artifact/sign behavior unless source-supported.
- Do NOT generalize beyond the specific source statement.
- Keep the card tight: 2-6 short lines max.

Recommended format:

High_Yield_Q:
"Ultrasound appearance pivot for [ENTITY]: what is the key sonographic appearance (as stated in source)?"

High_Yield_A:
- Sonographic feature: [source-stated appearance]
- Doppler / acoustic / compressibility feature: [only if explicitly stated]
- Exam pivot: [one line: why this helps recognize or discriminate]

If the ultrasound description is too long:
- Split into multiple High-Yield cards ONLY if each card is its own distinct exam pivot.
- Otherwise omit lower-value details.
