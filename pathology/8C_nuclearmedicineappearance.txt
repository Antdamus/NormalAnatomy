NUCLEAR MEDICINE APPEARANCE ENCODING RULE (MANDATORY WHEN SOURCE SUPPORTS)

If the provided Core/RadPrimer/STATdx source explicitly describes nuclear medicine appearance
(for example tracer uptake or non-uptake, photopenia, warm-versus-cold behavior, scintigraphic distribution, PET avidity, tracer-specific discriminator behavior, or source-named nuclear medicine pivots):

You MUST generate at least ONE dedicated High-Yield card encoding that nuclear medicine appearance.

(If Sectioned Build mode is active, this requirement is QUEUED and must be emitted only in SECTION G.)

Rules:
- Nuclear medicine details may be included ONLY if explicitly stated in the source.
- Include the tracer or exam name exactly as written when possible.
- Do NOT infer uptake, photopenia, avidity, washout, or physiologic distribution unless source-supported.
- Do NOT generalize one tracer's behavior to another tracer.
- Do NOT convert source silence into a negative finding.
- Keep the card tight: 2-6 short lines max.

Recommended format:

High_Yield_Q:
"Nuclear medicine appearance pivot for [ENTITY]: what is the key tracer-based appearance (as stated in source)?"

High_Yield_A:
- Tracer / exam: [source-stated tracer or study]
- Appearance: [source-stated uptake, photopenia, or other behavior]
- Exam pivot: [one line: why this helps recognize or discriminate]

If the nuclear medicine description is too long:
- Split into multiple High-Yield cards ONLY if each card is its own distinct exam pivot.
- Otherwise omit lower-value details.
