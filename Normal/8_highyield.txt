CARD TYPE: NORMAL HIGH-YIELD

SECTION PURPOSE

This is the default compact concept card for non-image-first normal content.

-----------------------------------------------------

TRIGGER CONDITION

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD exists only when:
- High_Yield_Q is populated

If High_Yield_Q is populated, the note must also contain:
- High_Yield_A

Question must remain blank.

If any other trigger field or front-driving prompt family is populated:
- the note is structurally invalid

-----------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO USE NORMAL HIGH-YIELD

Use it for compact, source-supported teaching points such as:
- anatomy definitions
- landmark rules
- normal appearance anchors
- approach rules
- review sequence
- normal checklist items
- measurement or morphology rules when explicitly supported
- normal variant anchors
- concise "what makes this normal" teaching points
- visibility classification when operationally useful
- orientation-anchor cards
- concise landmark-based localization rules for less-visible structures
- anatomy-first stroke or focal-lesion localization anchors when source-compatible
- compact reverse-localization cards that teach what region or compartment is being localized from visible landmarks
- compact synthesis review cards when they create a stronger reading framework

This is the preferred home for exam-approach content when image recognition is not the primary task.

It is also the main bridge between source anatomy content and practical radiology identification when the card does not need an image front.

-----------------------------------------------------

-----------------------------------------------------

FRONT-SIDE BLINDING

Front should contain only the fields required by the shared template for this card type.

High_Yield_Q should:
- ask for one compact learning target
- stay direct and answer-specific
- avoid embedded answers
- avoid turning into a paragraph prompt

When both forms are source-supported, prefer fronts that train practical localization or review navigation over fronts that only ask for a bare list of structures.

Examples of stronger front forms:
- "Which landmarks should anchor review of..."
- "How do you localize..."
- "What is the safest way to place..."
- "Which visible structures help identify..."
- "If a finding is centered here relative to these anchors, what space or compartment are you localizing to?"

-----------------------------------------------------

HIGH-YIELD ANSWER RULE

High_Yield_A should be compact, structured, and operational.

Good content forms:
- one-sentence definition
- short checklist
- short ordered review sequence
- compact modality anchor
- concise normal-vs-variant separator
- concise visibility statement
- short landmark-based localization rule
- short "localize this by first identifying..." rule

Preferred answer shape by modality when source-supported:
- MRI/CT: visible anchor -> relative position -> visibility honesty -> practical placement rule
- Ultrasound: echogenic or interface anchor -> probe plane or sweep -> continuity or branching clue -> practical placement rule

When clinically useful and source-supported, High_Yield_A may end with one compact line explaining why this localization matters for real interpretation.

Do NOT turn High_Yield_A into an unstructured paragraph dump.

When localization support is relevant, High_Yield_A should ideally include:
- the primary visible landmarks
- the best plane or modality when source-supported
- the relative position language that makes localization practical
- a concise rule that distinguishes directly seen from topographically inferred

For ultrasound-focused topics, High_Yield_A may instead or additionally emphasize:
- the key echogenic anchor
- the lumen, wall, or interface that should be identified first
- branching or continuity clues
- the probe plane or sweep that best establishes the relationship

If the answer names multiple structures or compartments, organize them around a usable imaging map rather than a flat recitation list.

For example, prefer grouping by:
- anchor region or level
- visible landmark first, then less-visible related structure
- supratentorial versus infratentorial review path

over a bare unordered catalogue.

If the card still reads like an inventory after this translation, it should usually be merged, rewritten, or omitted.

-----------------------------------------------------

EXAM-APPROACH RULE

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD is the preferred card type for:
- landmark checklist
- normal workflow anchors
- review sequence
- what constitutes a complete normal review
- concise overcall-prevention checklist items
- orientation-anchor cards
- localization cards for structures that are mainly inferred rather than crisply seen

If the source gives a reason why the rule works:
- that reason may be better encoded separately as NORMAL MECHANISM

-----------------------------------------------------

HIGH-YIELD SAFETY CHECK

Before export, confirm:
- High_Yield_Q is populated intentionally
- High_Yield_A is populated
- High_Yield_Q is the only front-driving prompt family
- Question is blank
- no other trigger fields are populated
- no forbidden fields are populated
- Image_Annotated is blank unless Image is populated
- Original_Caption is blank unless Image is populated
- the answer is concrete and source-supported
- if the card is broad or overview-style, it still improves practical structure identification rather than only testing memorized enumeration
