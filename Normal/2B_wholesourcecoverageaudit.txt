SECTION PURPOSE

This file forces whole-source review and prevents under-extraction of normal-anatomy articles.

-----------------------------------------------------

WHOLE-SOURCE COVERAGE RULE

Before generating cards, review the full source article and audit all card-worthy content buckets.

Do NOT stop after extracting only:
- one named structure
- one variant
- one mimic
- one figure caption
- one summary paragraph

Normal-anatomy articles often contain multiple independent teaching buckets.
The engine must audit all of them.

-----------------------------------------------------

COVERAGE AUDIT CHECKLIST

For each full RadPrimer or STATdx article, explicitly check whether the source supports any of the following:

1) named structures
2) spatial relationships
3) landmark identifiers
4) modality-specific normal appearance anchors
5) exam approach or review-order rules
6) relevant planes, sequences, or phases
7) completeness checklist items
8) measurement or morphology concepts
9) normal variants
10) pseudolesions or mimics
11) pitfalls or overcall risks
12) discriminator points between normal, variant, and abnormal
13) mechanism-style explanations for normal appearance
14) board-relevant pivots or classic traps
15) visibility status of key structures (directly visible, variably visible, or mainly topographically inferred)
16) practical landmark-based localization rules
17) orientation anchors that should be identified first
18) lesion- or stroke-localization-relevant adjacencies when anatomy-first and source-compatible

Every bucket must be checked.
Only source-supported buckets may generate cards.

-----------------------------------------------------

COMPLEXITY ASSESSMENT RULE

After the whole-source bucket audit, classify the article workflow as either:
- one-shot appropriate
OR
- staged generation preferred

Use staged generation when the article is:
- long
- multi-bucket
- multi-modality
- image-heavy
- rich in variants, mimics, and pitfalls
- dependent on a review sequence or extraction map to preserve quality

Do not default every full article to one-shot generation.

-----------------------------------------------------

POST-AUDIT HANDOFF RULE

After bucket audit:
- hand off pedagogic ordering to 2F_masteryprogressionrules.txt
- hand off workflow mode choice to 2G_adaptivegenerationrules.txt

Coverage audit is not complete until:
- all relevant buckets have been checked
- article complexity has been assessed
- the generation mode has been chosen intentionally

-----------------------------------------------------

NO PATHOLOGY-COLLAPSE RULE

Do not collapse a normal-anatomy article into pathology-style output just because it mentions disease mimicry.

If the source is still fundamentally teaching normal content, keep the output centered on:
- normal recognition
- normal review strategy
- variant recognition
- pitfall resistance

-----------------------------------------------------

FIGURE CAPTION RULE

Do not let a single figure caption dominate the extraction.

Image cards may use figures, but the card set must still reflect the full article when the workflow requires full-source review.

-----------------------------------------------------

COVERAGE-TO-CARD RULE

Checking a bucket does NOT mean forcing a card.

Generate a card only when the bucket contains:
- a distinct learning target
- enough specificity to support a clean card
- a fit within the allowed card types

If a bucket lacks enough support, omit the card rather than invent details.

-----------------------------------------------------

AUDIT FAILURE RULE

If the source was not reviewed in full, or if major supported buckets were ignored:
STOP.
Do not generate cards.
