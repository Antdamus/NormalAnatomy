NORMAL ULTRASOUND APPEARANCE ENCODING RULE

SECTION PURPOSE

This module governs ultrasound-specific normal anatomy, normal appearance, variants, and pitfalls in the NORMAL engine.

It does not create a new card type.
Ultrasound content must be routed into existing card families.

-----------------------------------------------------

TRIGGER CONDITION

Apply this module when the source explicitly describes or demonstrates ultrasound-specific content, including:
- normal echogenicity or echotexture
- wall, lumen, interface, fascial plane, cortex, medulla, tendon, nerve, vessel, or duct appearance
- posterior acoustic behavior
- Doppler flow pattern or vascularity
- compressibility, mobility, reducibility, peristalsis, or dynamic maneuver
- probe position, plane, sweep, window, or graded-compression approach
- normal variant or physiologic appearance on ultrasound
- artifact or pseudolesion that may be mistaken for disease
- ultrasound-specific normal-vs-abnormal discriminator

Do not force ultrasound cards when the source only mentions ultrasound as a possible modality without describing a usable appearance, approach, or pitfall.

-----------------------------------------------------

CARD-TYPE ROUTING

Route ultrasound content as follows:

NORMAL UNKNOWN
- image-based recognition of a normal structure, relationship, interface, or variant on ultrasound

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD
- expected normal sonographic appearance
- first-pass ultrasound landmark or sweep rule
- compact checklist for finding a structure
- Doppler or dynamic maneuver rule when the source supports it

NORMAL MECHANISM
- why a normal structure has a specific echogenicity, shadowing, enhancement, Doppler pattern, or dynamic behavior
- why a probe plane or maneuver demonstrates the relationship
- may use approved external mechanism clarification when the source states the appearance but gives a thin explanation

NORMAL BOARDS TRAP
- normal ultrasound mimics, artifacts, pseudolesions, or variants that can be overcalled

NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL
- true ultrasound separator between normal/variant/pseudolesion and pathology

-----------------------------------------------------

ULTRASOUND CONTENT RULE

Ultrasound cards should preserve the source-stated modality details.

When source-supported, include:
- echogenicity or echotexture
- key visible anchor
- interface, wall, lumen, or layer relationship
- posterior acoustic feature
- Doppler feature
- dynamic behavior or maneuver
- probe plane, window, or sweep
- one practical interpretation pivot

Do not infer ultrasound properties from anatomy alone.

Do NOT infer:
- Doppler flow
- compressibility
- mobility
- posterior enhancement
- shadowing
- twinkle, ring-down, comet-tail, reverberation, anisotropy, mirror artifact, or other artifact behavior
- probe maneuver or patient positioning

unless the source states it or it is being used under the approved external mechanism clarification rule and is clearly labeled.

-----------------------------------------------------

ULTRASOUND QUESTION SHAPING RULE

Prefer direct, modality-specific fronts:
- "On ultrasound, what normal appearance is expected for [structure]?"
- "Which ultrasound landmark should anchor localization of [structure]?"
- "What probe sweep or plane best confirms [relationship]?"
- "Which ultrasound feature prevents overcalling [normal variant/pseudolesion] as pathology?"
- "What Doppler or dynamic feature supports the normal interpretation?"

Avoid vague fronts:
- "What does ultrasound show?"
- "What did the source say about ultrasound?"
- "Describe the ultrasound findings."

-----------------------------------------------------

ULTRASOUND ANSWER SHAPE

Keep answers compact and operational.

Recommended structure:
- Anchor: [first visible structure or interface]
- Appearance: [source-stated echogenicity/texture/wall/lumen/layer feature]
- Maneuver / Doppler / acoustic feature: [only if source-supported]
- Interpretation pivot: [how this helps identify normal anatomy or avoid overcall]

For dynamic ultrasound, preserve the action:
- probe compression
- Valsalva
- position change
- swallowing
- breathing
- flexion/extension
- active or passive motion

For Doppler, preserve the type when stated:
- color Doppler
- power Doppler
- spectral Doppler
- arterial versus venous waveform
- expected flow direction or absence of flow

-----------------------------------------------------

ULTRASOUND SAFETY CHECK

Before export, confirm:
- ultrasound details are source-supported or clearly labeled under the approved external mechanism clarification rule
- the card teaches one ultrasound retrieval target
- the front does not leak the answer
- the answer does not become a generic anatomy paragraph
- Doppler, acoustic, dynamic, and artifact claims are not invented
- disease mimics remain bounded to normal-engine overcall prevention or true discriminator teaching
