NORMAL NUCLEAR MEDICINE APPEARANCE ENCODING RULE

SECTION PURPOSE

This module governs nuclear medicine and molecular imaging content in the NORMAL engine.

It covers normal biodistribution, physiologic uptake, normal variants, pseudolesions, tracer-specific pitfalls, and source-supported normal-vs-abnormal discriminators.

It does not create a new card type.
Nuclear medicine content must be routed into existing card families.

-----------------------------------------------------

TRIGGER CONDITION

Apply this module when the source explicitly describes or demonstrates nuclear medicine content, including:
- PET, PET/CT, PET/MRI
- SPECT or SPECT/CT
- planar scintigraphy
- bone scan
- HIDA scan
- renal scan
- thyroid/parathyroid scan
- tagged red blood cell scan
- white blood cell scan
- sulfur colloid scan
- MIBG, Octreoscan, DOTATATE, PSMA, FDG, NaF, sestamibi, iodine, gallium, or other named tracer behavior
- physiologic uptake or excretion
- normal biodistribution
- tracer-specific expected non-uptake
- normal variants or pseudolesions
- disease mimics created by physiologic uptake or altered flow
- nuclear medicine discriminator features

Do not force nuclear medicine cards when the source only names a nuclear study without describing a usable appearance, uptake pattern, biodistribution, or pitfall.

-----------------------------------------------------

CARD-TYPE ROUTING

Route nuclear medicine content as follows:

NORMAL UNKNOWN
- image-based recognition of physiologic uptake, normal distribution, normal excretion, or a normal variant when the figure supports it

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD
- expected tracer distribution
- normal uptake or non-uptake pattern
- physiologic excretion route
- compact tracer-specific normal checklist
- normal variant or pseudolesion anchor

NORMAL MECHANISM
- why a tracer localizes normally to a tissue, organ, excretory pathway, or physiologic process
- why a normal variant or pseudolesion creates uptake, photopenia, or altered distribution
- may use approved external mechanism clarification when the source states the pattern but gives a thin explanation

NORMAL BOARDS TRAP
- physiologic uptake or normal biodistribution that may be mistaken for disease
- tracer-specific pseudolesion
- altered normal flow or excretion pattern that creates a false lesion impression

NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL
- true separator between physiologic uptake/variant/pseudolesion and pathology

-----------------------------------------------------

NUCLEAR MEDICINE CONTENT RULE

Nuclear medicine cards must be tracer-specific.

When source-supported, include:
- exact tracer or exam name
- expected physiologic uptake, excretion, distribution, or non-uptake
- phase or timing if stated
- planar versus SPECT/SPECT-CT versus PET/PET-CT context when relevant
- normal variant or pseudolesion pattern
- one practical interpretation pivot

Do not generalize one tracer's behavior to another tracer.

Do NOT infer:
- uptake intensity
- avidity
- photopenia
- washout
- retention
- delayed activity
- physiologic distribution
- excretion route
- sensitivity, specificity, or performance

unless explicitly stated by the source or clearly labeled under an approved external clarification rule.

Do not convert source silence into a negative nuclear medicine finding.

-----------------------------------------------------

NUCLEAR MEDICINE QUESTION SHAPING RULE

Prefer direct, tracer-specific fronts:
- "What physiologic uptake pattern is expected for [tracer/exam]?"
- "Which normal biodistribution pattern prevents overcalling disease on [study]?"
- "For [structure/process], what nuclear medicine appearance is expected with [tracer]?"
- "What tracer-specific pitfall can mimic disease in [region]?"
- "What feature separates physiologic uptake from pathology on [study]?"

Avoid vague fronts:
- "What did nuclear medicine show?"
- "What did the source say about PET?"
- "Describe the scan."

-----------------------------------------------------

NUCLEAR MEDICINE ANSWER SHAPE

Keep answers compact and operational.

Recommended structure:
- Tracer / exam: [exact source-stated study]
- Normal pattern: [uptake, non-uptake, photopenia, excretion, biodistribution, or variant]
- Timing / phase: [only if source-supported]
- Interpretation pivot: [how this prevents overcall or supports normal recognition]

For physiologic uptake, identify the normal tissue/process when source-supported:
- metabolism
- blood pool
- hepatobiliary excretion
- renal excretion
- osteoblastic activity
- reticuloendothelial uptake
- iodine trapping/organification
- receptor expression

If the mechanism is not clear in the article but the pattern is source-supported, use NORMAL MECHANISM with the approved external-source label rather than hiding the outside explanation in an unlabeled answer.

-----------------------------------------------------

NUCLEAR MEDICINE SAFETY CHECK

Before export, confirm:
- the tracer or exam is named when the source names it
- the card teaches one nuclear medicine retrieval target
- physiologic uptake and pathologic uptake are not blurred
- the answer does not generalize across tracers
- performance metrics are not fabricated
- outside mechanism support is explicitly labeled when used
- disease mimics remain bounded to normal-engine overcall prevention or true discriminator teaching
