CARD TYPE: NORMAL MECHANISM

SECTION PURPOSE

This card type explains why a normal appearance looks the way it does.

-----------------------------------------------------

TRIGGER CONDITION

NORMAL MECHANISM exists only when:
- Mechanism is populated

If Mechanism is populated, the note must also contain:
- Mechanism_Q
- Entity_Label

Question must remain blank.

If any other trigger field or front-driving prompt family is populated:
- the note is structurally invalid

-----------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO USE NORMAL MECHANISM

Use NORMAL MECHANISM only when the source explicitly supports an explanation such as:
- anatomic basis of a normal appearance
- developmental basis of a normal variant
- physiologic basis of an expected appearance
- modality-specific reason a structure appears a certain way
- reason a sequence, plane, or phase best demonstrates a normal concept
- reason a discriminator or review step works
- reason a landmark-based localization rule works

Do NOT use this card for routine naming or definition recall.

-----------------------------------------------------

FIELD MATRIX

Required:
- Clinical_Context
- Entity_Label
- Mechanism_Q
- Mechanism

Conditionally allowed:
- Image only when the explanation truly depends on a figure or comparison
- Image_Annotated when annotated support is necessary
- Imaging_Differentiation
- Original_Caption only when image workflow requires it
- summary when required by source hierarchy

Forbidden:
- Question
- Most_Likely_Diagnosis
- Differential_Q
- Differentials
- Boards_Trap_Q
- Boards_Trap
- High_Yield_Q
- High_Yield_A
- Radiopaedia_Link
- Radiopaedia_Case_Context
- Radiopaedia_Case_Summary
- Radiopaedia_Case_Differential

-----------------------------------------------------

FRONT-SIDE BLINDING

Front should contain only the fields required by the shared template for this card type.

Mechanism_Q should:
- be direct
- ask why or how
- focus on causal understanding
- avoid repeating the answer in the wording

Do not leak the explanation on the front.

-----------------------------------------------------

MECHANISM CONTENT RULE

Mechanism should explain one causal or explanatory teaching point clearly and compactly.

Valid targets:
- why fluid, signal, enhancement, or contour looks normal on a given modality
- why a sequence or phase improves confidence
- why a variant has its typical appearance
- why a landmark or relationship is reliable
- why one visible anchor is safer than pretending the target itself is clearly seen

Avoid:
- pseudo-mechanisms
- vague statements like "because of anatomy"
- speculative physiology
- long essays

-----------------------------------------------------

EXAM-APPROACH MECHANISM RULE

Exam-approach content belongs here only when the source gives a reason.

Examples:
- why portal venous phase is the best phase for a normal relationship
- why a given MRI sequence clarifies a normal structure
- why starting with a certain landmark reduces mislocalization

If the source gives only the rule without the reason:
- use NORMAL HIGH-YIELD instead

-----------------------------------------------------

MECHANISM SAFETY CHECK

Before export, confirm:
- Mechanism is populated intentionally
- Mechanism_Q is populated
- Mechanism_Q is the only front-driving prompt family
- Entity_Label is populated
- Question is blank
- no other trigger fields are populated
- no forbidden fields are populated
- Image_Annotated is blank unless Image is populated
- Original_Caption is blank unless Image is populated
- the explanation is explicitly source-supported
