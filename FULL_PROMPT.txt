==============================================================================

SECTION PURPOSE

This file defines what counts as the target entity in the NORMAL engine.
It prevents normal-anatomy content from being mislabeled as disease content.

-----------------------------------------------------

TARGET ENTITY TYPES (ALLOWED)

In this engine, the target entity may be any of the following:

1) Structure
- a named organ, vessel, duct, nerve, ligament, muscle, bone, joint, fascial plane, space, or compartment

2) Region
- an anatomic territory or portion of a structure
- examples: pancreatic head region, renal sinus, posterior fossa cisterns

3) Landmark
- a named imaging landmark used to orient a normal study
- examples: portal vein bifurcation, SMA-SMV relationship, quadrigeminal cistern

4) Physiologic Appearance
- an expected normal appearance caused by normal physiology
- examples: enhancement pattern, signal intensity pattern, normal layering, expected fluid or gas distribution when source-supported

5) Normal Variant
- a named or clearly described non-pathologic variant appearance
- examples: prominent column of Bertin, junctional cortical defect, persistent fetal lobulation

6) Pseudolesion or Mimic
- a normal structure, contour, fold, interface, vessel, artifact, or physiologic state that may simulate disease

7) Modality-Specific Normal Concept
- a normal concept that only makes sense in relation to modality, sequence, plane, or phase
- examples: normal corticomedullary differentiation, expected T1/T2 relationship, expected bowel wall appearance on a specific phase

8) Exam-Approach Concept
- a review-order rule, orientation anchor, landmark checklist, sequence priority, phase priority, or completeness rule for reading a normal exam

-----------------------------------------------------

ENTITY LABEL RULE

Entity_Label must store the true learning target as a concise noun phrase or concept label.

Entity_Label may contain:
- the structure name
- the variant name
- the pitfall name
- the landmark name
- the normal appearance concept
- the exam-approach concept

Entity_Label must NOT contain:
- long explanation text
- multiple unrelated entities
- filler
- unsupported synonyms
- a fake diagnosis label for a pure normal card

-----------------------------------------------------

ENTITY RESOLUTION RULE

Before writing a card, determine which entity type is being taught.

If the source is mainly teaching:
- "what this is" -> structure, region, landmark, variant, or mimic
- "what it should look like" -> physiologic appearance or modality-specific normal concept
- "how to review the study" -> exam-approach concept
- "why it looks that way" -> modality-specific normal concept or physiologic appearance, usually for NORMAL MECHANISM

Do not default every card to a diagnosis-style entity.

-----------------------------------------------------

VARIANT VS MIMIC RULE

Do not merge these categories automatically.

- A normal variant is a real non-pathologic anatomic or developmental alternative.
- A pseudolesion or mimic is a normal appearance that can be mistaken for pathology.

Some entities may qualify as both.
If so, the card must clearly state which educational role is being tested.

-----------------------------------------------------

EXAM-APPROACH ENTITY RULE

Exam-approach concepts are valid target entities in this engine.

They include:
- first landmark to identify
- sequence in which to review a study
- plane/sequence/phase that best establishes a normal relationship
- minimum normal checklist for a complete review
- maneuver or discriminator that prevents overcalling disease

These are not new card types.
They are entities that must be mapped into the existing allowed card types.

-----------------------------------------------------

ENTITY SAFETY RULE

If the source does not clearly support a named entity or concept:
- leave Entity_Label blank only when the card type can function without it
OR
- omit the card

Do not invent an entity name.

- just because it exists
- to make a card feel more complete
- to mirror another card type
- to avoid a blank

Only populate a field when the intended card type explicitly allows it.

-----------------------------------------------------

FRONT-SIDE BLINDING RULE

Fronts must contain only the elements required by their specific template.

No front may include:
- answer leakage
- named diagnoses unless the card intentionally requires a tightly bounded mimic-rejection anchor
- caption-derived answer statements
- management clues
- interpretive hints that collapse the task

All fronts must preserve:
- anatomy recognition
- normal-pattern recognition
- normal-vs-variant discrimination
- pitfall resistance

-----------------------------------------------------

GLOBAL INTERPRETIVE RULE

These card types exist to train NORMAL material, not pathology-first diagnosis.

In this engine:

NORMAL UNKNOWN
- identify a normal structure, landmark, relationship, expected normal appearance, physiologic appearance, or normal variant from image review

NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL
- distinguish normal vs abnormal, variant vs pathology, or mimic vs lesion using the dominant discriminator

NORMAL MECHANISM
- explain the anatomic, developmental, physiologic, modality, sequence, or phase-based reason a normal appearance or discriminator works

NORMAL BOARDS TRAP
- encode classic normal mimics, pseudolesions, overcalls, omission traps, and normal variants commonly mistaken for pathology

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD
- encode compact anatomy definitions, landmark rules, approach rules, modality anchors, checklist items, normal measurements when source-supported, and variant anchors

-----------------------------------------------------

EXAM-APPROACH MAPPING RULE

Exam-approach content must stay inside the existing card types.

Preferred mappings:
- review sequence, landmark checklist, completeness rules -> NORMAL HIGH-YIELD
- why a phase, sequence, or plane helps -> NORMAL MECHANISM
- common overcall or omission errors -> NORMAL BOARDS TRAP
- image-based anchor landmark identification -> NORMAL UNKNOWN
- true discriminator tasks -> NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL

Do not invent a new approach card type.

-----------------------------------------------------

ARCHITECTURE SAFETY RULE

The same field can only mean one trigger at a time.

Therefore:
- never populate Differentials unless intentionally generating NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL
- never populate Mechanism unless intentionally generating NORMAL MECHANISM
- never populate Boards_Trap unless intentionally generating NORMAL BOARDS TRAP
- never populate High_Yield_Q unless intentionally generating NORMAL HIGH-YIELD
- never populate Question for a non-NORMAL-UNKNOWN image card

This rule is absolute.

- import compatibility

- preserve annotated and non-annotated pairing exactly

- localization of a variant or mimic

VALUE FORMATTING RULE

Output values must preserve shared import compatibility.

Therefore:
- do not insert extra columns
- do not emit placeholder tokens such as N/A
- use blank fields when content is intentionally absent
- keep per-field formatting controlled and deterministic
- do not use literal newline characters inside TSV fields
- use <br> where internal formatting is required

For summary specifically:
- use <br> only
- no literal newline characters

-----------------------------------------------------

PRE-TSV VALIDATION GATE

Before export, all of the following must be true.

A) Trigger integrity
- NORMAL UNKNOWN requires Image and Question
- if Image is populated but Question is blank, the note must not be treated as NORMAL UNKNOWN
- any non-NORMAL-UNKNOWN note with Image must keep Question blank
- NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL exists only when Differentials is populated
- NORMAL MECHANISM exists only when Mechanism is populated
- NORMAL BOARDS TRAP exists only when Boards_Trap is populated
- NORMAL HIGH-YIELD exists only when High_Yield_Q is populated
- no dual-trigger note may be exported

B) Required-field integrity
- if Differentials is populated, Differential_Q and Imaging_Differentiation must be populated
- if Mechanism is populated, Mechanism_Q and Entity_Label must be populated
- if Boards_Trap is populated, Boards_Trap_Q and Entity_Label must be populated
- if High_Yield_Q is populated, High_Yield_A must be populated

C) Forbidden-field integrity
- UNKNOWN must leave all non-UNKNOWN trigger fields blank
- DIFFERENTIAL DRILL must leave Question, Mechanism, Boards_Trap, and High_Yield_Q blank
- MECHANISM must leave Question, Differentials, Boards_Trap, and High_Yield_Q blank
- BOARDS TRAP must leave Question, Differentials, Mechanism, and High_Yield_Q blank
- HIGH-YIELD must leave Question, Differentials, Mechanism, and Boards_Trap blank

D) Source and summary integrity
- whole-source review requirements must be satisfied before card generation
- summary must never substitute for full-source review
- if a card originates from RadPrimer or STATdx, summary must be populated
- if a card does not originate from RadPrimer or STATdx, summary must be blank
- summary must remain article-only with no Core mixing, no outside facts, and no unsupported inference

E) Front-side blinding
- front fields must preserve the intended cognitive task
- no front may leak the answer through wording, captions, or extra fields
- image-containing non-UNKNOWN cards must keep Question blank

F) Field hygiene
- blank is better than fake content
- do not export filler
- do not export N/A
- do not export pseudo-diagnoses
- do not export fake differentials
- do not export pseudo-mechanisms
- do not export unsupported claims

If any gate fails:
- do not export TSV
- fix the violating note or split the note into separate card types

-----------------------------------------------------

NORMAL DIFFERENTIAL DRILL ENFORCEMENT

For every note where Differentials is populated:
- Differential_Q must be populated
- Imaging_Differentiation must be populated
- Question must be blank
- Mechanism_Q and Mechanism must be blank
- Boards_Trap_Q and Boards_Trap must be blank
- High_Yield_Q and High_Yield_A must be blank unless the note is split into a separate card

If the note is image-grounded:
- Image may be populated
- Image_Annotated may be populated when needed

-----------------------------------------------------

IMAGE / QUESTION ENFORCEMENT

Question is reserved for NORMAL UNKNOWN.

Therefore:
- Image + Question is the UNKNOWN trigger pair
- Image without Question may support other card types
- Image + Question + any other trigger field is a structural failure

-----------------------------------------------------

TAG ARCHITECTURE RULE

Keep the existing shared tagging architecture.
Do not invent a separate tag schema just for the normal engine unless the wider system explicitly adopts it.

-----------------------------------------------------

PROMPT-BUILDER COMPATIBILITY RULE

The modular rule files must concatenate into FULL_PROMPT.txt in deterministic order.
The builder must fail loudly if a required section file is missing.

Do not collapse the modular system into one monolithic prompt blob.
