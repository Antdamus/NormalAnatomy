CORE_FACTS_USED:

* CTA is the gold standard / modality of choice for suspected traumatic aortic injury.
* Classic injury locations are relatively fixed aortic levels, especially the aortic isthmus; also root and hiatus.
* Direct CT signs include intimal irregularity/flap, pseudoaneurysm, dissection, and intramural hematoma.
* Mediastinal hemorrhage separated from an intact aorta by a fat plane can be presumed venous; hemorrhage contacting the aortic wall suggests aortic injury.
* Minimal aortic injury is approximately 10–30% of blunt traumatic aortic injuries in Core and involves only the intima.
* Mechanisms include rapid deceleration, shearing forces, water-hammer phenomenon, and osseous pinch.
* Chronic post-traumatic pseudoaneurysm may show peripheral calcification and thrombus.
* Open surgical repair is described in Core/IR as gold standard historically, with endovascular stent-graft repair increasingly performed.
