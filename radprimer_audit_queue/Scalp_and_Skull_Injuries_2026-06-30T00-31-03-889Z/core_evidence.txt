CORE_FACTS_USED:

- Complex skull fractures that are depressed, multiple, bilateral, or crossing sutures are suspicious for child abuse.
- Isolated linear skull fracture is nonspecific and can be seen in both abuse and accidental trauma.
- Bone scintigraphy is insensitive for skull fractures.
- CT is preferred for trauma fracture assessment when imaging is indicated; MRI is not the initial modality for fracture detection but can help with selected complications/soft-tissue or ligament questions.
