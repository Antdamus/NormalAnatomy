CORE_FACTS_USED:

- Traumatic aortic injuries occur at relatively fixed aortic levels: root, isthmus, and hiatus; isthmus is the most common location.
- CTA is the gold standard for suspected traumatic aortic injury.
- Direct CT signs include intimal irregularity, dissection flap, pseudoaneurysm, and intramural hematoma.
- Mediastinal hemorrhage separated from an intact aorta by a fat plane may be venous; hemorrhage contacting the aortic wall suggests aortic injury.
- Minimal aortic injury involves only the intima; common CT findings include intimal irregularity, small intimal flap <1 cm, no/little periaortic hematoma, intraluminal thrombus, or intramural hematoma.
- Post-traumatic pseudoaneurysm is a contained rupture; classic location is the aortic isthmus.
- Chronic pseudoaneurysm may have peripheral calcification and thrombus.
- IR section confirms CTA as gold standard and notes open repair historically/gold standard with endovascular stent-graft repair increasingly performed.
