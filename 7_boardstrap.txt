CARD TYPE: NORMAL BOARDS TRAP

SECTION PURPOSE

This card type captures normal findings that are commonly overcalled or misread.

-----------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO USE NORMAL BOARDS TRAP

Use NORMAL BOARDS TRAP for:
- normal mimics of disease
- pseudolesions
- variants mistaken for pathology
- structures commonly overcalled
- modality-specific normal misreads
- omission traps in reviewing a normal exam

This is a high-value card type in the NORMAL engine.

-----------------------------------------------------

REQUIRED FIELD SHAPE

Required:
- Clinical_Context
- Boards_Trap

Usually populated:
- Boards_Trap_Q
- Entity_Label
- Imaging_Differentiation

Conditional:
- Image when the trap is image-grounded
- summary when source is full RadPrimer or STATdx

Usually blank:
- Question unless intentionally NORMAL UNKNOWN
- Most_Likely_Diagnosis unless absolutely required for a bounded mimic rejection
- Differential_Q
- Differentials unless true discrimination is separately intended
- Mechanism_Q
- Mechanism
- High_Yield_Q
- High_Yield_A
- Radiopaedia fields

-----------------------------------------------------

TRAP CONTENT RULE

Boards_Trap must encode:
- what the trap is
AND
- what feature, check, maneuver, or landmark keeps the reader safe

Do not stop at naming the trap.

-----------------------------------------------------

QUESTION RULE

Boards_Trap_Q should ask for one of the following:
- the pitfall itself
- the key reason it is not pathology
- the safety check that prevents overcall

Do not write a broad essay prompt.

-----------------------------------------------------

OVERCALL-PREVENTION RULE

Imaging_Differentiation is strongly encouraged for this card type.
Use it to state the separator that prevents misdiagnosis.

Examples of valid separator categories:
- preserved normal anatomy
- lack of aggressive behavior
- expected course or attachment
- phase/sequence consistency
- stability of a normal interface

-----------------------------------------------------

TRAP SAFETY RULE

Do not invent classic traps.
Do not exaggerate rarity into a boards pearl unless the source supports the teaching value.
