## Fused Teaching Backbone

### Definition And Time Window

- [Both] Subacute subdural hematoma is a subdural collection about 3 days to 3 weeks old, located between arachnoid and dura or within the inner dural border cell layer.
- [Both] The collection contains partially liquefied clot and resorbing blood products surrounded by granulation tissue membrane.
- [Both] Trauma may be minor, especially in older adults, because bridging cortical veins are stretched or torn as they cross the subdural space.
- [Both] The hematoma can enlarge through repetitive hemorrhage from the outer membrane; membrane enhancement marks an unstable collection prone to rehemorrhage.

### CT Recognition Pattern

- [Both] On NECT, subacute SDH is crescentic, iso- to hypodense, extraaxial, and can spread diffusely over a hemisphere.
- [Both] It may be the same density as cortex. The search pattern is displaced gray-white interface, compressed sulci, and inwardly displaced dots of CSF rather than relying only on obvious hyperdensity.
- [Both] SDH may cross sutures but not dural attachments.
- [Both] Density evolves from acute hyperdensity toward isodensity at about 7-10 days and hypodensity around 10-14 days; recurrent hemorrhage can create mixed density.
- [STATdx] STATdx images 3 and 4 add alternate axial and coronal right-sided examples of difficult isodense sSDH detection.
- [STATdx] STATdx images 9 and 10 form an atomic intracranial-hypotension cluster: bilateral nearly isodense SDHs plus sagittal CT signs of brain sagging and a plump pituitary.

### MR Recognition Pattern

- [Both] T1 signal is iso- to hyperintense; T2 is often hyperintense; FLAIR does not suppress like CSF.
- [Both] DWI signal varies with hematoma age and may show a double-layer appearance; a hyperintense band can indicate relatively fresh bleeding from the outer membrane.
- [STATdx] Add SWI/GRE to trauma/hemorrhage MR protocols; the source package specifically broadens the protocol advice from GRE to SWI/GRE.
- [Both] T1 C+ may show enhancing membranes. Outer membrane enhancement supports late/unstable sSDH and helps distinguish sSDH or chronic SDH from pachymeningeal mimics.
- [Both] MR is more sensitive than CT for SDH and for additional traumatic brain injuries.
- [STATdx] STATdx images 11-15 provide an atomic multimodality cluster demonstrating CT, T1, FLAIR, T2, and T1 C+ behavior of subacute collections.
- [STATdx] STATdx images 16 and 17 add early/fluid-level and DWI examples, including the caution that blood signal on DWI is not always predictable.

### Differential Diagnosis

- [Both] Other subdural collections include effusion, hygroma, and empyema.
- [Both] Pachymeningopathies can mimic thickened dura; actively search for intracranial hypotension signs when trauma history is absent.
- [Both] Chronic dural sinus thrombosis remains in the differential for subdural-type collections and dural/pachymeningeal findings.
- [Both] Signal on MR often parallels intracerebral hematoma evolution, but exceptions occur, especially in early subacute hematomas.

### Pathology And Clinical Context

- [Both] Etiology centers on bridging vein injury; the source packages also describe a delayed hypersensitivity component with antigen release during clot liquefaction and antigen-presenting cells in the neomembrane.
- [Both] Gross pathology is granulation tissue with resorbing blood products. The outer membrane is tied to enlargement and repetitive hemorrhage; the inner membrane is tied to liquefaction of SDH.
- [Both] Presentation can be subtle and delayed, including headache, seizure, or focal neurologic deficit.
- [Both] Demographics include young patients and older adults, with male predominance in the source article.
- [STATdx] STATdx image 18 adds an infant nonaccidental-trauma scenario, preserving the pediatric/abuse context as a distinct recognition example.

### Diagnostic Checklist

- [Both] Initial screen is NECT; consider contrast-enhanced CT for membranes or loculations.
- [Both] On MR, include FLAIR, DWI, and SWI/GRE for hemorrhage or trauma indications.
- [Both] If sSDH appears without a trauma history, evaluate coagulation status and search for intracranial hypotension: brain sagging, dura-arachnoid thickening, engorged dural venous sinuses, and plump pituitary.
- [Both] Report mass effect, sulcal compression, midline shift/herniation if present, bilateral balance, mixed density suggesting rebleeding, and membrane enhancement.
