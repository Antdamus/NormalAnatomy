## Fused Teaching Backbone

### Core Pattern

- [Both] The key nuclear medicine finding is a cortical defect on Tc-99m DMSA renal cortical imaging caused by acute pyelonephritis, renal cortical scar, infarct, mass, cyst, or a normal anatomic variant.
- [Both] Pyelonephritis-related photopenic defects tend to extend toward the renal hilum and usually lack cortical concavity; chronic cortical scars are typically more superficial and may show cortical contour loss.
- [Both] Upper-pole involvement is common for pyelonephritis and reflux-related scarring, followed by lower pole and then mid pole.
- [Both] DMSA cortical scintigraphy detects more cortical defects than ultrasound or IV urography and remains the nuclear medicine reference test for cortical scar/infarct assessment.
- [STATdx] STATdx adds that acute pyelonephritis on DMSA must be interpreted with the clinical fever/infection context, and that contrast-enhanced CT is commonly the practical standard for acute pyelonephritis because it is fast and widely available.

### DMSA Technique And Interpretation

- [Both] Tc-99m DMSA binds to cortical proximal convoluted tubules; delayed imaging around 2 hours highlights functioning renal cortex.
- [Both] Pediatric DMSA activity is 40-50 microCi/kg with a minimum of about 350 microCi; adult activity is about 5 mCi IV.
- [Both] Posterior and anterior planar images are acquired with low-energy parallel-hole collimation. Differential renal function is calculated from geometric mean counts, using kidney and background regions of interest.
- [Both] SPECT provides the best three-dimensional cortical evaluation; if SPECT is unavailable, anterior, posterior, and bilateral posterior oblique views help characterize defects.
- [STATdx] STATdx specifically notes that DMSA SPECT/CT can reduce false-positive SPECT and false-negative planar interpretations, and that pinhole collimation may be considered when SPECT is unavailable.
- [Both] Poor renal function can require delayed imaging to permit longer uptake time. Renal tubular acidosis can increase urinary excretion, and renal failure can increase hepatic clearance.

### Acute Pyelonephritis And Scar Evolution

- [Both] Acute pyelonephritis may show a striated DMSA uptake pattern that extends toward the hilum.
- [Both] In first-time acute pyelonephritis within the first week, DMSA abnormalities occur in roughly 50-79% of cases; ultrasound can miss many of these defects.
- [Both] Acute pyelonephritis-related cortical defects can resolve by about 6 weeks. Late follow-up at 12-24 months may show resolution of about half of defects seen by 6 months or earlier.
- [Both] Persistent defects after acute infection should prompt evaluation for correctable structural causes, including VUR or abnormal bladder dynamics.
- [Both] Pyelonephritis and reflux-induced scarring are clinically important because they predispose to proteinuria, hypertension, and eventual renal failure.

### Vesicoureteral Reflux And Management-Relevant Imaging

- [Both] VUR is common in children with first-time pyelonephritis and renal scars, and scar burden correlates with higher reflux grade.
- [Both] DMSA cortical scanning is indicated in high-grade reflux, commonly grade 3 or higher, and reflux with recurrent UTI.
- [Both] New renal cortical scars despite antibiotic prophylaxis support surgical or endoscopic correction rather than watchful waiting.
- [Both] Low-grade reflux can be managed conservatively because many cases resolve spontaneously; high-grade reflux or reflux with scars is more likely to require intervention.
- [Both] After VUR correction, DMSA can evaluate differential renal uptake and residual scars.

### Labeled Leukocyte And Gallium Scans

- [Both] In-111 WBC scanning can identify acute pyelonephritis in complicated cases because native kidneys normally do not show In-111 WBC uptake.
- [STATdx] STATdx adds that In-111 WBC is not indicated in children because of poor spleen dosimetry.
- [Both] Tc-99m HMPAO WBC can normally show renal and bladder activity, reducing sensitivity and specificity for pyelonephritis; STATdx notes it can be used in children.
- [Both] Ga-67 citrate is sensitive but nonspecific for acute pyelonephritis. Normal symmetric renal uptake can persist up to 48 hours after injection.
- [Both] Bilateral renal Ga-67 uptake beyond 48 hours can reflect renal insufficiency or interstitial nephritis and mimic diffuse acute pyelonephritis. Focal Ga-67 uptake can reflect acute pyelonephritis, lymphoma, leukemia, or metastases.
- [STATdx] Ga-68 PSMA PET has renal cortical uptake and is under investigation for renal cortical evaluation, with early studies correlating renal uptake volume and renal function tests.

### Correlative Imaging And Differential Diagnosis

- [Both] Contrast-enhanced CT can show acute or chronic changes; chronic scarring appears as focal cortical thinning. CT has similar sensitivity and specificity to DMSA but carries radiation and IV contrast risks.
- [Both] Ultrasound has limited sensitivity for renal scars but can show focal cortical thinning, echogenic cortex, or absent power Doppler signal.
- [Both] MR has moderate sensitivity and specificity for scars; coronal fat-suppressed T1 imaging is emphasized for scar detection.
- [Both] Renal masses and cysts can create focal absent DMSA uptake. Cysts tend to be discrete, sharp, and rounded.
- [Both] Polyarteritis nodosa can mimic acute pyelonephritis with a striated appearance. Renal infarcts may produce peripheral wedge-shaped defects acutely and contour abnormality chronically.
- [STATdx] STATdx adds several normal-variant pitfalls: interrenicular septum, junctional parenchymal defect, and intense column of Bertin, in addition to fetal lobulation and splenic impression.

### Protocol Pitfalls And Quality Control

- [Both] A distended or neurogenic bladder may require catheterization to prevent back-pressure effects and interference from retained collecting-system activity.
- [Both] Patulous or obstructed collecting systems may need furosemide or delayed imaging up to 24 hours after tracer injection.
- [Both] Too much air in the tracer reaction vial can degrade the Tc-99m DMSA complex, causing increased renal uptake and increased hepatic background activity.
- [Both] For known or suspected horseshoe kidney, anterior imaging can be helpful.
- [STATdx] Any DMSA photopenic region should be described by intensity, number, size, location, shape, margin distinctness, and associated contour abnormality. Normal split function is about 50:50 to 45:55; values outside that range suggest renal pathology and can be associated with high VUR risk.
