CARD TYPE: NORMAL HIGH-YIELD

SECTION PURPOSE

This is the default compact concept card for non-image-first normal content.

-----------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO USE NORMAL HIGH-YIELD

Use it for compact, source-supported teaching points such as:
- anatomy definitions
- landmark rules
- normal appearance anchors
- approach rules
- review sequence
- normal checklist items
- measurement or morphology rules when explicitly supported
- normal variant anchors
- concise "what makes this normal" teaching points

This is the preferred home for exam-approach content when image recognition is not the primary task.

-----------------------------------------------------

REQUIRED FIELD SHAPE

Required:
- Clinical_Context
- High_Yield_Q
- High_Yield_A

Usually populated:
- Entity_Label

Often useful:
- Imaging_Differentiation
- summary when source is full RadPrimer or STATdx

Conditional:
- Image if a figure materially improves the concept, but keep Question blank

Usually blank:
- Question
- Most_Likely_Diagnosis
- Differential_Q
- Differentials
- Mechanism_Q
- Mechanism
- Boards_Trap_Q
- Boards_Trap
- Radiopaedia fields

-----------------------------------------------------

HIGH-YIELD QUESTION RULE

High_Yield_Q should ask for one compact learning target:
- definition
- landmark
- checklist item
- review-order rule
- key normal appearance anchor
- variant anchor

Do not combine multiple unrelated asks in one prompt.

-----------------------------------------------------

HIGH-YIELD ANSWER RULE

High_Yield_A should be compact, structured, and operational.

Good content forms:
- one-sentence definition
- short checklist
- short ordered review sequence
- compact modality anchor
- concise normal-vs-variant separator

Do NOT turn High_Yield_A into an unstructured paragraph dump.

-----------------------------------------------------

EXAM-APPROACH RULE

NORMAL HIGH-YIELD is the preferred card type for:
- landmark checklist
- normal workflow anchors
- review sequence
- what constitutes a complete normal review
- concise overcall-prevention checklist items

If the source gives a reason why the rule works, that reason may be better encoded separately as NORMAL MECHANISM.

-----------------------------------------------------

HIGH-YIELD SAFETY RULE

Do not create a high-yield card from vague general advice.
The answer must rest on a concrete source-supported rule.
